# Socoeur — Content System Strategy

This document explains how the Socoeur content production system is structured.

It describes:

- the workflow used to produce social media content
- the tools and stack used
- the organization between planning, production and publishing
- the role of AI in the process

The objective is to maintain a consistent, scalable and lean content production system.

---

# Philosophy

The system is designed around four principles:

1. **Consistency over volume**
2. **Image-first content**
3. **Lean production**
4. **Human-led creative direction**

AI is used to assist production and structure thinking, but not to replace creative direction.

---

# Global Workflow

The workflow follows a structured pipeline:

Idea → Scripted → Ready to Shoot → Shot → Edited → Ready → Published

Each content idea moves through these stages.

The operational tracking is done in a **Google Sheet Content OS**.

This sheet functions as the production cockpit.

---

# Production Structure

Content production is organized in **sprints** aligned with the product launch timeline.

Sprint 1 — Cultural Tease  
Sprint 2 — Fragment Reveal  
Sprint 3 — Official Reveal  
Sprint 4 — Launch Mode

Each sprint has a specific narrative objective.

---

# Content Formats

Three recurring content formats structure the content production.

Urban Presence  
Architecture-driven compositions with strong symmetry.

Material Focus  
Macro details of the product, fabric and textures.

Controlled Motion  
Subtle movement and editorial pacing designed for Reels.

These formats ensure visual consistency across content.

---

# Batch Production System

Content is produced using a **batch production approach**.

Instead of producing posts individually, content is filmed in sessions that generate material for multiple posts.

Typical batch session:

30–40 minutes shooting  
45 minutes editing

One session should produce assets for 4–6 posts.

---

---

# AI Integration

AI is integrated as a **structuring and creative support tool**.

AI is used for:

- generating scripts
- generating captions
- exploring creative directions
- maintaining editorial consistency
- organizing the content system

AI is not used to replace real-world photography or generate the core brand imagery.

The visual identity remains rooted in real environments and real photography.

# Visual Production

Content relies primarily on real-world environments.

Key visual elements:

Architecture  
Concrete  
Symmetry  
Neutral tones  
Natural light

The jersey color (red) acts as the primary color accent.

Two main shoots support the launch:

Studio Shoot — April 6  
Product and detail content.

Outdoor Shoot — April 12

---

# Publishing Rhythm

Target cadence:

1–2 posts per week

Content mix:

60% photos  
40% reels

Photos build brand identity.  
Reels expand reach.

---

# Long-Term Objective

The system is designed to support:

- the World Cup 2026 launch
- future collections
- scalable content production

The goal is to build a recognizable visual identity for Socoeur while maintaining a lean and flexible workflow.
