# Socoeur — Brand Guidelines

---

## Color Palette

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Concrete Grey | #E6E6E3 | Primary background, light surfaces |
| Off White | #F9F9F7 | Secondary background, packaging |
| Urban Charcoal | #1F1F1F | Text, dark elements |
| Asphalte Grey | #3A3A3A | Supporting dark tone |

Red is used exclusively on the garment.
Red must never appear in backgrounds, text, UI elements, or decorative contexts.

---

## Typography

Typeface: **Satoshi**

Weights in use:
- Satoshi Regular
- Satoshi Medium

No other weights. No bold. No light.

---

## Editorial Text Rules

- No uppercase in editorial contexts (captions, on-screen text, body copy)
- Titles and section headers may use title case
- Punctuation: minimal, intentional
- No exclamation marks
- No emojis

---

## Logo

**Wordmark**
- Socoeur in Satoshi Medium
- No final period
- Always in Urban Charcoal (#1F1F1F) or Off White (#F9F9F7) depending on background
- Never distorted, rotated, or placed on busy backgrounds

**Icon**
- SV monogram (secondary use only)
- Used when the wordmark would be too large or on small formats (favicon, apparel label)

---

## Visual Rules

- Red belongs only to the garment. Never use red elsewhere.
- Neutral tones dominate all compositions.
- Symmetry is preferred in architectural compositions.
- Soft, warm-natural light. No harsh flash or studio overexposure.
- Text on screen is rare — max 3–5 words when used.
- Clean framing. No visual clutter.

---

## What to Avoid

- Swiss clichés: mountains, watches, flags, neutrality references
- Hype streetwear aesthetics
- Aggressive color use
- Loud typography
- Busy compositions
- Nostalgic or folkloric references
