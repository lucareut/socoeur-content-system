# Socoeur — Content Plan (World Cup 2026)

## Context

Brand: Socoeur
Positioning: Cultural fashion brand referencing football
Tagline: Different roots, same colors

Key objective:
Launch the brand and first jersey capsule leading up to the World Cup 2026.

Limited stock: ~300–500 jerseys.

Target audience:
Urban Switzerland (Geneva, Lausanne, Fribourg, Zurich, Basel, Bern)
Age: 18–50
Lifestyle: street culture, design, football affinity but not hardcore fans.

---

## Philosophy

The system is designed around four principles:

1. **Consistency over volume**
2. **Image-first content**
3. **Lean production**
4. **Human-led creative direction**

AI is used to assist production and structure thinking, but not to replace creative direction.

## Sprint Structure

### Sprint 1 — Cultural Tease

Period: Mid-March → Early April
Goal: Introduce the visual universe. No jersey reveal.

Content elements:
- White football
- Architecture
- Concrete
- Symmetry
- Packaging teaser
- Minimal motion

Formats: Symbolic Tease + Urban Presence + Material Focus

Publishing frequency: 1–2 posts per week
Total posts: ~5 (SC-001 → SC-005)

---

### Sprint 2 — Fragment Reveal

Period: April 6 → April 20
Goal: Reveal fragments of the jersey.

Content elements:
- Fabric texture
- Stitching
- Silhouette
- Material details

Publishing frequency: 2 posts per week
Total posts: ~4

---

### Sprint 3 — Official Reveal

Period: April 21 → May 1
Goal: Reveal the full jersey.

Content elements:
- Models wearing jerseys
- Architecture compositions
- Portraits
- Walking shots
- Lifestyle visuals

Publishing frequency: 2–3 posts per week
Total posts: ~5–6

---

### Sprint 4 — Tournament Mode

Period: May → World Cup
Goal: Drive awareness and sales.

Content elements:
- Lifestyle content
- Fans wearing jerseys
- Pop-up activation
- Match moments
- Influencer content

Publishing frequency: ~2 posts per week

---

## Content Mix

60% photos
40% reels

Photos build brand identity.
Reels expand reach.

---

## Content Formats

**Urban Presence**
Structured architectural composition. Minimal movement. Strong symmetry.

**Material Focus**
Macro details. Texture and fabric. Neutral background.

**Controlled Motion**
Subtle movement. Clean editorial pacing. Designed for Reels.

**Symbolic Tease**
Anticipation without product reveal. Used primarily in Sprint 1.

---

## Batch Production System

Content is produced in sessions that generate material for multiple posts.

Typical batch session:
- 30–40 minutes shooting
- 45 minutes editing

One session → 4–6 posts.

## Operational Tracking
